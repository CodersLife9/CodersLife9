First Method

Gettings Your Nails Ready

Step 1

(Get your supplies together.)
To give yourself a gorgeous manicure, make sure you have all the right supplies. You might have to spend a bit up front, but next time you want to paint your nails, you'll already be covered. Get the following products

  • Nail polish remover
  • Cotton balls or cotton swabs
  • Nails Buffer
  • Nails trimmers
  • Nails file
  • Cuticle or hand cream
  • Nail Polish
  • Basecoat
  • Top coat

Step 2

Set up your workspace
Nail polish and remover can damage many surfaces such as cloth, wood finish and plastic. Wear an expendable t-shirt and no valuable accessories, sit at a desk or table, and protect it with scrap paper (not newsprint, which smudges). Make sure the table itself and anything near it is not particularly valuable or important to keep perfect because there might be a spatter or spill soaking through. You might not want to work near a computer, for instance.

Step 3

(Remove your old nail polish)
Use nail polish remover and cotton balls or swabs. Some kinds of nail polish remover may dry the areas on your nails and around them. You may wish to find one that doesn't do this as much, but do not be concerned unless you have a severe allergic reaction. If you have and wish to keep fake nails, such as acrylic, choose a polish that will not remove them, and don't let it soak much. Unless you use it once a month or less, don't use a nail polish remover with acetone in it. Though acetone will make removing the nail polish easier, it can damage the nails themselves.

Step 4

(Cut and file your nails.)
Use nail clippers and trim your nails. Don't cut them too short; you should be able to see at least a little of the white tip still, all the way across. Using a nail file, file the nail and create a smooth and clean shape. Gently drag the file across the nail, rather than pushing it. Excessive force or sawing back and forth will weaken the nails and cause them to break.Pivot the hand with the file through each stroke to make a smooth curve rather than angles. Do not file them too short: just clean up any points or roughness left by the clippers.

  • If you want to remove fake nails, perhaps because they look odd from having grown out too far, here's how to do that.
  • Do not round the corners down into the sides of the nail bed. This can cause the nail to become ingrown. Be particularly careful with the big toe, which, perhaps due to shoes, is more prone to ingrowing.

Step 5

(Buff your nails)
Using a white block of a stick-type nail buffer, or a pad-type nail buffer and buffing powder, buff the surface of the nail a little bit to even out the surface and to smooth out ridges. Remember not to buff too much; thinning it too much will weaken it.[4] Perfect edge-to-edge flatness is not practical or necessary. A soft, flexible buffer will more easily buff the sides of the nail along with the middle. You may wish to buff your nails after pushing back the cuticles if there is some residue where the cuticles used to cover, in order to scrape or grind it off in the process. Being thin, soft, and not firmly attached, it should come off easily.

Step 6

(Soak your nails.)
Get a bowl or stopper the sink and fill it with warm (not hot!) water and a few drops of soap. Soak your hands only for a few minutes. The water and soap will help to loosen dirt, dead skin, and any dust left over from filing and buffing, and soften cuticles. Use a nail brush to gently clean your nails and the skin around them. Gently scrape under your nails if necessary to remove dirt. If you want your nails and cuticles really manageable, you can soak them in Dawn dishwasher soap

Step 7

(Prepare your cuticles.)
Dry your nails and apply a cuticle cream. Using a cuticle pusher, also called an orange stick, gently push back the cuticle. Do not force them back, and never cut your cuticles. Even if the equipment is sterile, removing the cuticle can cause infection and leaves the then-less-protected margin of the skin vulnerable. Wipe off the excess cream with a tissue or towel in the direction that you pushed.

  • A small binder clip is great for pushing back cuticles. Make sure it's clean and well-formed, without sharp edges. Fold the wire grips to lay flat against each other. Hold the clip between the thumb and forefinger or middle finger by the flat metal sides, with the grips passing between in the direction of the little finger tip; the flat back piece extending past the thumb and forefinger. Now you're ready to push back the cuticles on the other hand (switch later to do the one currently holding the clip).

Step 8

Apply hand cream or lotion.
Take a lotion or hand cream and massage it into your hands. If you have very dry skin use an intensive lotion, if not, any lotion will do. Be sure to rub it into and around your nails and let it soak in for 30 minutes or longer.

  • This is as well done after painting the nails and allowing them to dry thoroughly as before painting. For very dry skin, apply some greasy lotion and sleep with cheap cotton gloves over your hands to allow it to remain on and work for a long time without keeping you from productive activities.
  • Nail polish/lacquer will not stick to nails with moisturizer on them, so take a Q-tip dipped in nail polish remover and quickly wipe the nails to remove the lotion. Wipe excess remover off promptly to mitigate nail damage.

Medical Manicure

Performing a medical manicure involves special care to ensure the health and safety of the nails and surrounding skin. Here's a basic method for a medical manicure:

Materials Needed

  1. Nail clippers
  2. Nail file
  3. Cuticle pusher
  4. Cuticle trimmer
  5. Buffing block
  6. Gentle exfoliating scrub
  7. Moisturizing lotion or oil
  8. Antiseptic solution
  9. Cotton balls
  10. Warm water
  11. Towel
Procedure:
  1. Prepare the Work Area:
    • Ensure that your tools and the work surface are clean and sanitized.
    • Have all the necessary materials within reach.
  2. Remove Nail Polish (if applicable):
    • Use a non-acetone nail polish remover to gently remove any existing nail polish.
  3. Trim and Shape Nails:
    • Use nail clippers to trim the nails to the desired length.
    • Shape the nails using a nail file. File in one direction to prevent nail damage.
  4. Soak Nails:
    • Soak the client's hands in warm, soapy water for about 5-10 minutes. This helps to soften the nails and cuticles.
  5. Push Back Cuticles:
    • Use a cuticle pusher to gently push back the cuticles. This should be done carefully to avoid causing any damage.
  6. Trim Cuticles:
    • Trim any excess or dead cuticle using a cuticle trimmer. Be cautious not to cut too much, as this can lead to infection.
  7. Exfoliate Hands and Nails:
    • Apply a gentle exfoliating scrub to the hands and nails, massaging in circular motions. This helps remove dead skin cells.
  8. Buff Nails:
    • Use a buffing block to smooth the surface of the nails. This also helps in promoting blood circulation to the nail bed.
  9. Cleanse and Disinfect:
    • Cleanse the nails and surrounding skin with an antiseptic solution to prevent infection.
  10. Moisturize:
    • Apply a generous amount of moisturizing lotion or oil to the hands and nails. Massage it in to promote hydration.
  11. Final Touch:
    • If the client desires, apply a clear nail polish or a nail strengthening treatment for added protection.
  12. Educate the Client:
    • Provide aftercare instructions to the client, emphasizing proper nail and cuticle care to maintain the health of their nails.

Remember, if you are not a licensed professional, it's important to follow local regulations and consider referring individuals to a qualified nail technician or healthcare professional for specific medical conditions or concerns.

Acrylic Manicure

Creating an acrylic manicure involves a series of steps, and it's essential to follow proper procedures for a professional and long-lasting result. Here's a simple method for doing an acrylic manicure:

Materials Needed:
  1. Acrylic nail kit (includes acrylic powder, acrylic liquid, and acrylic brushes)
  2. Nail primer
  3. Nail tips or forms
  4. Nail file and buffer
  5. Cuticle pusher
  6. Nail dehydrator
  7. Acetone or nail polish remover
  8. Paper towels or lint-free wipes
  9. UV or LED lamp (optional)
  10. Nail polish and top coat (optional)
Procedure:
  1. Prepare the Nails:
    • Remove any existing nail polish using acetone or nail polish remover.
    • Shape the nails using a nail file according to the client's preference.
    • Gently push back the cuticles using a cuticle pusher.
  2. Apply Nail Tips (if using):
    • Select the appropriate size nail tips for each finger.
    • Apply a small amount of nail glue to the tips and press them onto the natural nails.
    • Trim and shape the tips to the desired length.
  3. Trim and Shape Acrylic Forms (if using):
    • If using acrylic forms, trim them to fit the natural nails.
    • Apply the forms under the natural nails, ensuring they are secure.
  4. Dehydrate and Prime:
    • Apply a nail dehydrator to remove excess oils and prepare the nails for acrylic application.
    • Apply a thin layer of nail primer to promote adhesion.
  5. Mix Acrylic Powder and Liquid:
    • Dip the acrylic brush into the acrylic liquid and then into the acrylic powder.
    • Create a bead of acrylic by placing the brush near the cuticle area and gently pressing the brush to release the bead onto the natural nail or nail tip.
  6. Sculpt the Acrylic:
    • Use the brush to shape and mold the acrylic on the nail.
    • Work quickly but precisely to achieve the desired shape and thickness.
    • Repeat this process for each nail.
  7. Shape and Buff:
    • Shape the acrylic nails using a nail file to achieve the desired length and shape.
    • Smooth the surface with a buffer for a polished finish.
  8. Cleanse and Polish (optional):
    • Cleanse the nails using a lint-free wipe and nail cleanser.
    • Optionally, apply nail polish and top coat for a finished look.
  9. Moisturize:
    • Finish by moisturizing the cuticles and hands.
Remember to follow safety guidelines and use proper ventilation when working with acrylic products. Additionally, practice good hygiene by sanitizing your tools before and after each use.

French Manicure

Performing a French manicure involves creating a classic and elegant look with a natural base and white tips. Here's a step-by-step method to achieve a French manicure:

Materials Needed:
  1. Base coat
  2. Natural or nude-colored nail polish
  3. White nail polish or French manicure striping tape
  4. Clear top coat
  5. Nail polish remover
  6. Cotton swabs or precision brushes
  7. French manicure pen (optional)
  8. Nail file and buffer
Procedure:
  1. Start with Clean Nails:
    • Remove any existing nail polish using a non-acetone nail polish remover.
    • Shape the nails using a nail file and gently push back the cuticles.
  2. Apply Base Coat:
    • Apply a thin layer of base coat to protect the natural nails and improve polish adhesion.
    • Allow the base coat to dry completely.
  3. Apply Natural or Nude Polish:
    • Apply one or two coats of a natural or nude-colored polish as the base. Allow each coat to dry thoroughly before applying the next.
  4. Create the White Tips:
    • Option 1: Use French Manicure Striping Tape
    • Place French manicure striping tape just below the tips of the nails to create a guide for the white polish.
    • Apply white nail polish above the tape, creating a clean and even line.
    • Allow the white polish to dry before carefully removing the striping tape.
    • Option 2: Freehand with White Polish
    • If you have a steady hand, freehand the white tips using white nail polish or a French manicure pen.
    • Start at the center of the nail and extend towards the sides, creating a natural-looking curve.
    • Allow the white polish to dry completely.
  5. Clean Up Edges:
    • If needed, use a precision brush or cotton swab dipped in nail polish remover to clean up any uneven edges or mistakes.
  6. Apply Top Coat:
    • Once the white polish is dry and well-defined, apply a clear top coat to seal and protect the entire nail.
    • The top coat will add shine and enhance the longevity of the manicure.
  7. Final Touches:
    • Allow the top coat to dry completely before performing any additional activities.
    • Optionally, apply cuticle oil or moisturizer to keep the cuticles and surrounding skin hydrated.
Enjoy your classic and timeless French manicure! Remember to take your time during each step to achieve a professional-looking result.
